Whats New

  • New school gym

    The loan for the construction costs is to be granted with a repayment period of 50 years, according to a government statement on Tuesday. The primary school should be able to use the hall for the same length of time without incurring any costs. The government announced the plans for the joint project in February 2016.
    At the Rudolf Steiner School, the gymnasium is part of a larger building project that includes classrooms and a canteen in addition to a new kindergarten. Of the total costs of CHF 20.8 million, including loans, CHF 14.7 million have been secured so far. According to the notification, the cantonal loan is approved subject to proof of financing for the entire cost.
    If the project goes well, the gym could go into operation at the beginning of the 2022/2023 school year. According to the government, this would mean that the cooperation project would be implemented more quickly than a separate new building on the Bruderholz school site.
    Due to the extension of the primary school by two years due to a cantonal school reform, twelve instead of eight classes have been taught in the Bruderholz primary school since the summer of 2014. The existing gym is therefore overcrowded.
